Rawin Chhay

If you don't try, you will never know.

26 July 2020

Being a pharmacist is one of the most things that I proud the most in my life. As a girl moving from province to the capital city to pursue a degree is not easy for me, Cambodian women are traditionally expected to be modest, soft-spoken, light walkers, well-mannered, industrious, belong to the household, act as the family's caregivers. I need to break the family's rules and require a lot of encouragement and motivation to start this journey. Besides, in Cambodia, there's still some criticism about being a pharmacist student, like why don't you choose to study doctor instead of this? You spend 5 to 6 years just to become a person that can only sell the medicine? IS IT REALLY WORTH IT? Maybe what people said is true. I just add two 2 more years, and I will become a doctor, but people have a different thought and different desire.

In my perspective, after experiencing 10 years in the field, the pharmacist is more than just selling the medicine. We collaborating with other healthcare professionals to plan, monitor, review, and evaluate patient effectiveness. Moreover, Pharmacist students must remain up to date with changes in their profession, which may contain new practice guidelines, a new pharmacy and therapeutic products, and new technologies. On top of that, I can work independently, and the job itself is flexible for me. Before the University started, I have expected that study about medicine is just about to remember the name of the drug and its effectiveness. In contrast, after getting into the field, everything is not like what I thought. Studying this major is hard for me. It requires me a lot of patience and memorizes skills. I can say if you love to study drug metabolism, veterinary Pharmacy, or even conducting pharmaceutical research career in Pharmacy may be for you.

My advice to the junior is: if you are in the medical faculty, you should participate in community services, volunteering programs, (serving and helping others), health education services and local health care organizations. Pharmacist students must have both a passion and proficiency for these subjectsâ€”both are essential to making informed decisions on proper dosage, utilization, and distribution of medications to people and animals alike. If you stuck in the first few years, no worries everything will start to make sense when you get the internship. "Be patient, great things take time."
