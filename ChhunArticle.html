What Medical student think about their field.

Stay positive, Work hard, Make it happen.

1 August 2020

As a medical student, it required more effort that you put in doing somethings that you have ever done in your life, but chances are, it also includes more fun than you ever had before. There are various delusions about being a medical student. Nevertheless, it is fun, thrilling and profoundly rewarding, especially for what you are doing. Being a Medical student is not what I desire. Back in the past, I was hallucinating about becoming an agriculturist. However, after experienced studying in that field for a few months, I found that I am not into it. Furthermore, my auntie gives me some suggestion about becoming a doctor and finally, I decided to apply for a prestigious medical university in Cambodia. People may question me why you study what you don't love? Then, ask yourself, why we always do what we love, and why not choose what suits us the most?

After four years in the university, I feel comfortable in what I have learnt and appreciated the rules as a medical student. This career is not only a service to the society but also one that will give you respect. This field is for the ones who love to challenge themselves every day. One needs to possess strong decision-making skills to shape a career in medicine. Real-time thinking and rightly employing knowledge is an essential part of a medical practitioners' job; one small mistake can affect someone's life to a great extent. Compassion towards humanity, more reliable belief system and desire to bring a positive change are the driving forces for finding success in the world of medicine. You will be able to use what you learn for the rest of your life. This might seem like a relatively trivial point, but it should not be neglected. The fact for many programs is that you are only really studying to pass your exams and once you have managed this the information which you have tried so hard to learn is mainly useless to you. This is not the case in medicine, with areas of study including anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, pharmacology and pathology all being directly applicable in diagnosing, understanding and treating disease. Not only is this a great incentive to learn the core course material well, so that you will be a competent doctor, it is also an inspiration to go beyond the primary lecture material and satisfy your curiosity about what you have been taught. As a medic, this extra detail could one day be put into practice in a clinical situation and could make a crucial difference to a patient. When you are studying medicine, you are not merely studying for the following exam but taking the first steps on a course of lifelong learning, building your basis of professional knowledge throughout your medical career. In my opinion, reading is vital as a medic. Every material you read must be trustable from reliable sources; otherwise, it may cause consequences in the future.

Last but not least, for those who want to apply for medical school in Cambodia. I suggest that they should have prepared the Language like English or French if you're going to enter the University of Health and Science because there's still some class conducted in French. Base on my experience, there is a lot of article in English than French. If you are not good at Language, don't worry, you still can learn along the way.
